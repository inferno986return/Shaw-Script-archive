FriedOrange’s Quikscript keyboard layout (rationale)

Many Quikscript letters have fairly well-associated Latin counterparts; the following mappings should come naturally to all English speakers. See how the traditional “short” vowels are unshifted (lower case), and their “long” counterparts shifted (upper case):

                           
p b t d k g f v s z j y w h m n l r i e a o u I E A O U

Most of the remaining letters are usually associated with digraphs (pairs of letters) in the Latin alphabet, such as <th>, <sh>, <ch>, <wh>, <ng>, and <ll>. To these we may add the voiced equivalents <dh> and <zh>. Shifted keys are used for these mappings where the unshifted form was already taken:

        
T D S Z c W N C L

· and · are treated as variants of <k> and <g>, both to emphasise the voiced/unvoiced distinction and to save the X key for additional vowels. The Q and X keys may now be given to the two pairs of vowel letters that don't fit anywhere else. The last remaining letter, , is assigned to the Y key as it resembles the Greek upsilon and is adjacent to U (which was already used twice):

      
K G q Q x X Y

Finally, every good Quikscript or Shavian keyboard layout needs a method of typing the angled brackets  and/or namer dot ·; I chose to access these using the right Alt key (AltGr on UK keyboards or either Option key on the Mac) along with the 9, 0, and . keys.

Shavian adaptation

The Quikscript letters      have no equivalents in Shavian, but Shavian does have an additional eight “compound letters” not present in Quikscript. The best available keyboard mappings (that make some kind of sense) would probably be to put ·𐑸 on the R key; ·𐑹 on W since it is next to Q (·𐑷); ·𐑳 on J since it is below U; and ·𐑿 on H since it is near both of its constituent letters, Y U (·𐑘 ·𐑵). I simply allocated the remaining five letters to spare shifted keys, in groups of two and three alongside each other:

J R W H F G C V B
