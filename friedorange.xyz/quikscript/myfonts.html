My fonts

Here are the fonts I have created. They were all completed using the free font editor, FontForge. Each font meets or exceeds my own suggested standard for glyph inclusion, QSGL Level II. This means they possess the basic symbols, numerals and punctuation required for writing English, in addition to the four supplementary Quikscript letters î™©, î™ª, î™« and î™¬. The Quikscript characters are mapped to the Unicode Private Use Area, so youâ€™ll need to install a special keyboard layout to type with them. Finally, the fonts are all available under the SIL Open Font License 1.1. This means they may be freely used, distributed, extended and adapted.

These fonts are distributed in three formats: SFD for editing in FontForge; TTF for installation on computers; and WOFF for embedding in Web pages.

  • Screen is like most â€˜pixelâ€™ fonts that use connected, square dots like the displays of 8-bit home computers and game consoles from the 1980s.
  • Print is made up of discrete circular dots, resembling the output of a dot-matrix printer or â€˜best before/use byâ€™ stamps on food products.

  • Video is essentially an interpolated version of Print. It resembles the on-screen displays of VCRs, Teletext, camcorders and the like; harking back to the early days of computerisation in television and home video.
