Learn

These documents contain a wealth of information about Quikscript. They are intended as self-teaching guides and may be used to learn almost all there is to know about the alphabet. The rest involves using Quikscript on computers, which is covered on the other pages of this site.

Foreword, Introduction, Notes on the Spelling, and Suggestions for Writing
From The Shaw Alphabet Edition of Androcles and the Lion, pp. 9-16 & 143-152
By C.R. Sopwith, Sir James Pitman, Peter MacCarthy, and Kingsley Read, 1962

This excerpt from Androcles and the Lion pertains to the original Shavian (Shaw) Alphabet. It explains the context behind its creation and the methodology of spelling used in the transliteration. It also provides advice for newcomers that should still be helpful to Quikscript enthusiasts.

Read

Writing circle letter
From Kingsley Read, dated 18th December 1969

This letter consists of four pages of Senior Quikscript, handwritten by Kingsley Read, so the style matches that of the Quikscript Manual. In it, he mainly discusses the Welsh orthography and the board game Go, amongst replies to group members on other topics. I found it archived on the Quikscript Yahoo! Group, with somewhat low-resolution, monochrome images that were heavily affected by JPEG compression. I cleaned it up as much as possible before making it available here.
